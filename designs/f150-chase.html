MJD Chase Light – 10th Gen F-150 (1997–2003)

Desert-tested chase light system with configurable LED pods and integrated brake/running lights.

This CAD-designed chase light replaces your stock third brake light, offering chase functionality while maintaining an OEM-style fit. The housing is 3D printed in ASA for heat and UV resistance, and mounts in the factory third-brake-light location on 1997–2004 Ford F-150s. A wiring diagram and install notes are provided. Basic wiring and modifications are required on installation.

What’s Included

  • 1× 3D-printed ASA chase light housing in black.
  • 2× 3" 40 W LED pods in the lens/beam option you select above.
  • 2 compact LEDs for brake lights and 1 compact LED for running lights, mounted in the housing.
  • Foam gasket material to seal the housing to the cab.

What You’ll Need to Supply

  • 12 V power, ground, and tap into your third-brake and running light circuits.
  • Switch, fuse, and relay for the chase pods (recommended on their own fused circuit).
  • Optional: strobe controller module if you want chase/strobe patterns on the amber pods.

Recommended Electrical Setup

I recommend running the chase pods on their own dedicated 12 V wiring harness with an in-line fuse, switch, and strobe controller. On my own truck, the amber chase pods are connected to a switch panel with a built-in fuse/relay and an additional strobe controller module to wirelessly enable chase patterns. The brake and running lights are connected by tapping into the factory third-brake and running light circuits. More info is coming soon on wiring diagrams and detailed setup suggestions.

Choose Your Lens Configuration

All options use the same LED lights — you’re choosing the lens and beam pattern. The Yellow Amber Diffused lens can be seen installed on the chase light in the above product image. Yellow Amber Combo is the default setup if you don’t pick a specific option. The Diffused honeycomb lens is arguably the best chase light lense — they’re designed for maximum presence, not maximum distance. If you are looking for further light throw, the spot or combo lenses are better choices.
