Chase Light – 10th Gen F-150 (1997–2003)

Desert-tested chase light with configurable LED pods and integrated brake/running lights.

This chase light shown above is outfitted with the Yellow-Amber Honeycomb Diffused lens option.

This CAD-designed chase light replaces your stock third brake light, offering chase functionality while maintaining an OEM-style fit. The housing is 3D printed in ASA for heat and UV resistance, and mounts in the factory third-brake-light location on 1997–2004 Ford F-150s. A wiring diagram and install notes are provided. Basic wiring and modifications are required on installation.

What’s Included

  • 1× 3D-printed ASA chase light housing in black.
  • 2× pre-installed 40 W LED pods in the lens/beam option you select below.
  • 2x LEDs for brake and 1x LED for running lights, pre-installed in the housing.
  • Pre-installed foam gasket material to seal the housing to the cab.

What You’ll Need to Supply

  • 12 V power, ground, and tap into your third-brake and running light circuits.
  • Switches, fuse, and relay for the chase pods (recommended on their own fused circuit).
  • Optional: strobe controller module if you want chase/strobe patterns on the chase pods.

Recommended Electrical Setup

We recommend running the chase pods on their own dedicated 12 V wiring harness with an in-line fuse, switch, and strobe controller. The truck shown with the installed chase light is running the chase lights to a switch panel with a built-in fuse/relay and an additional strobe controller module to wirelessly enable chase patterns. The brake and running lights are connected by tapping into the factory third-brake and running light circuits using wago connectors. More info coming soon on wiring diagrams and setup suggestions.

Choose Your Lens Configuration

All options use the same 3" 40 W LED pods — you’re choosing the lens and beam pattern. Yellow Amber Combo is the default setup if you don’t pick a specific option. The Diffused lens is one of the best options for optimal chase light functionality — they’re designed for maximum presence, not maximum distance. If you want further distance lighting, consider the Spot or Combo lenses.
